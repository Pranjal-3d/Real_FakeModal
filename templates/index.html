📡 Frequency Domain Analysis
Digital screens have a precise matrix grid of pixels. When a camera recaptures a screen, the grid sampling creates high-frequency periodic interference (Moiré). The detector runs a 2D Hann-guided Fourier Transform (FFT) to isolate and measure these sharp frequency spikes.
🔍 Micro-Texture Profiling
Natural objects have stochastic, randomized textures. Screens, when photographed at close range, exhibit extremely uniform, grid-like micro-patterns. Local Binary Pattern (LBP) entropy calculations isolate these regularities to identify screen structures.
✴️ Edge Profile Outliers
Physical scenes captured by lenses yield edges governed by optics and lighting. Recaptured screen edges contain discrete step-functions of red/green/blue emitters. The Laplacian kurtosis features quantify these abnormal pixel gradient distributions.
